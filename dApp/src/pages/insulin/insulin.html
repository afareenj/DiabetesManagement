Insulin Dosage
Instructions

Long-acting insulin (Lantus) only needs to be injected once or twice daily. Insulin glargine works for 24 hours.

Regular prandial insulin (Humulin) should be injected 30 minutes before meals.

Rapid acting insulin (Novolog, Humalog) may be injected before or after eating. Dosage may vary depending on amount of food eaten because rapid insulin is given after meals.

Insulin dosage should be reduced when fasting for surgery, when not eating, or when hypoglycemia occurs. Illness or infection may increase insulin requirements.

Technique

It is import to insert the needle perpendicular to the skin. This ensures deep subcutaneous administration of insulin.

Dosage

Dosage may be adjusted based on the actual amount of food ingested for fast acting insulin.

For every 10-15 grams of carbohydrates consumed, use one unit of insulin dosage.

Example: If the food has 100g of carbohydrates, and 20g of dietary fiber, that would be approximately 100-20/2 = 90g total, which would be 9 units of insulin.
