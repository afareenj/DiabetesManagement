Info

Diabetes is a group of conditions that result in too much sugar (glucose) in the blood, usually associated with insulin, a hormone that normally controls blood glucose levels.

Type I:

Type I diabetes, or insulin-dependent diabetes, is an autoimmune condition that usually first presents during childhood. It is usually caused by the body’s own immune system attacking the insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas, causing little-to-no insulin to be produced.

Type II:

Type II diabetes is a far more common type of the disease, in which the body produces some insulin, but this amount is either not enough for the body’s needs or the body’s cells are resistant to the insulin. This is called insulin resistance, or lack of sensitivity to insulin.

General Symptoms to Expect

Common Symptoms:

Hunger or fatigue

Urinating more often

Being thirstier

Dry mouth and itchy skin

Blurred vision

Symptoms of Type I:

Unplanned weight loss

Nausea and vomiting

Symptoms of Type II:

Yeast infections

Slow healing cuts or sores

Pain or numbness in feet or legs

Health Behaviors

Make sure to keep a close watch on your blood sugar levels, and try to keep them at a healthy level set by your doctor. This will be done through a combination of medications, diet and exercise.

Keep up with dental care and oral health to prevent gum disease.

Do not smoke or use tobacco products to prevent further complications and disease complications such as heart disease and cancers.

Minimize alcohol use to improve overall health and well-being, which will in turn ease diabetes symptoms. Less alcohol use will help manage weight, improve heart health and increase quality of sleep.

Dangers

Call or visit a doctor if you...

Feel sick, weak, or very thirsty

Are urinating a lot

Have a bad stomach ache

Are breathing more deeply or faster than normal

Have sweet breath that smells like nail polish remover/acetone

Have pain or numbness in feet or legs
